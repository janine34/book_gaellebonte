About
I am Gaëlle, a student passionate about design, art and digital media. For the last two years, I have been studying design, art and media at Sorbonne university in Paris. More specifically my research focuses on web design, UI/UX and development.

In the past years, I worked for a photographer agency Agence Vu as editorial assistant and in a start-up incubator Creative Valley as assistant of the head of communication.

I am looking forward to new experiences in the field of digital design.

Turning point is a collection of Data and Information displayed in a visually appealing journey where we discover the factors and the threats of our sixth mass extinction of species. We decided to design our graphs with Illustrator to create a strong visual identity in black and white. Our website is constantly evolving as we dive deeper in the problem.  Through this journey you can discover and analyse the evolution of our nature and where we are now. Suddenly everything is changing in a very rapid pace yet where is the turning point ? Did we miss it? Are we at the end of the extinction or at the beginning? Did we miss our chance to save our planet?

IMU is a magazine about sustainable fashion and lifestyle. I worked on the visual identity, created the logo with linocut, I used a sustainable type named Ryman Eco which with its thin lines is supposed to reduce the use of ink by diffusing it through the spaces and fill them on the paper. Here, I chose to highlight the font and its spaces using it as a stamp, we can find it on the cover and chapter title in the magazine. For this first edition, I played on opacity on different kind of picture, to put in contrast plant, city and fabrics, to create a new kind of visual texture. On the content, I picked up articles and interviews from different media about fast fashion, ethic, sustainable fashion designer and societal topic about ecology.
